Eiffel Tower

A Timeless Icon of Paris

Introduction

  • Overview:The Eiffel Tower, completed in 1889 as the entrance arch to the 1889 World's Fair (Exposition Universelle), has become an enduring emblem of French culture and innovation. Standing at 324 meters (1,063 feet), it was the tallest man-made structure in the world until the completion of the Chrysler Building in New York City in 1930. The tower's design and construction were feats of engineering that defied conventional aesthetics of its time, ultimately cementing its place as one of the most visited and photographed monuments in the world.
  • Importance: The Eiffel Tower, a globally recognized symbol of Paris and a masterpiece of engineering, continues to captivate millions of visitors each year. This iron lattice tower, designed by Gustave Eiffel, stands as a testament to human ingenuity and artistic brilliance. In this blog, we'll explore the fascinating history, architectural marvels, and cultural significance of the Eiffel Tower.
